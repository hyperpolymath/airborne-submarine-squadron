Space Threats — What belongs in the outer dark?

Select everything that sounds fun. We'll refine from there.

A

Asteroid Belt

Dense debris field between Mars and Jupiter. Rocks of varying size — small ones chip hull on contact, large ones are navigational obstacles. Could contain salvageable resources or hidden enemy bases.

B

Solar Flares

Periodic radiation waves emanating from the Sun. Visible as a ring expanding outward — you have seconds to get behind a planet or take heavy damage. Forces orbit management around the solar cycle.

C

Enemy Patrol Fleets

Hostile warships in fixed orbital lanes. Engage if you enter their patrol radius. Harder enemies orbit closer to the sun — the inner system is a gauntlet.

D

Debris Clouds

Aftermath of destroyed ships or stations — slow-moving clusters of wreckage drifting in stable orbital paths. Can be scavenged for hull repairs or ammo, but dangerous to cross at speed.

E

Rogue Gravity Wells

Collapsed objects (neutron stars, micro black holes) lurking in the outer system. No visual warning — you feel the pull before you see the cause. Escaped only by burning hard and fast.

F

Planetary Radiation Belts

Van Allen-style bands around gas giants (Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus). Transiting them does increasing damage over time — you can cross them quickly or die slowly trying to orbit inside.

G

Space Mines

Proximity-triggered devices scattered in contested zones. Near asteroid belt, near enemy planets, or in chokepoints between SOI boundaries. Slow-drifting — hard to spot at speed.

H

Rogue Comets

Fast-moving objects on wildly elliptical paths crossing all orbital zones. Predictable if you watch their trajectory, lethal if you don't. Leave a debris trail behind them.
